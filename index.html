Parameters:

  • Infinite Length
  • Transverse Motion Only (i.e. only move in y-axis)

Newtons $2^{nd}$ Law $F = F_L + F_R$ mass over one $\Delta x ^{th}$ of string which can be expressed as $\Delta x ^{ths}$ worth of the linear mass density $\rho$ $a = \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial t^2}$

Deriving Quation of Motion

$\rho \Delta x \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial t^2} =\quad $ $F_R$ $ \qquad\quad + \quad$ $F_L$ $\rho \Delta x \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial t^2} = \quad$ $T_0\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\bigg\vert_{x_1 + \Delta x}$ $\quad + \quad F_L$ $\rho \Delta x \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial t^2} = \quad$ $T_0\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\bigg\vert_{x_1 + \Delta x}$ $\qquad - \quad$ $T_0\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\bigg\vert_{x_1}$
Flip it $F_R = T_0sin\theta_{x_1 + \Delta x} \quad$ ($sin\theta \approx tan\theta$ for small $\theta$) $F_R = T_0sin\theta_{x + \Delta x} \quad$ ($sin\theta \approx tan\theta$ for small $\theta$) $F_R = T_0sin\theta_{x + \Delta x} \quad$ We deduce: $\quad tan\theta = \frac{\partial y}{\partial x}$ $F_R = T_0\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\bigg\vert_{x_1 + \Delta x}$ and $F_L = -T_0\frac{\partial y}{\partial x}\bigg\vert_{x_1}$

\[ \begin{aligned} \require{color} \require{cancel} y(x, t) &= y(x)e^{i\omega t} \Rightarrow \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial t^2} = c^2\frac{\partial^2 y(x_1)}{\partial x^2} \\ y(x)(i\omega)^2e^{i\omega t} &= c^2\frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2}e^{i\omega t} \\ y(x)(-\omega)^2e^{i\omega t} &= c^2\frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2}e^{i\omega t} \quad (\div \ c^2e^{i\omega t} \text{ or } \times \frac{1}{c^2e^{i\omega t}}) \\ y(x)\frac{(-\omega)^2}{c^2}\cancel{e^{i\omega t}} &= \cancel{c^2}\frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2}\cancel{e^{i\omega t}} \\ y(x)\frac{(-\omega)^2}{c^2} &= \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2} \\ \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2} &= y(x)frac{(-\omega)^2}{c^2} \quad \text{Equation of Motion (Time Harmonic)} \\ \end{aligned} \]

Deriving Modes

Solutions for Time Harmonic

\[ \begin{aligned} \require{color} \require{cancel} \frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2} &= y(x)frac{(-\omega)^2}{c^2} \quad \text{Equation of Motion (Time Harmonic)} \\ y &= A e^{ikx} + B e^{ikx} \text {General Solution } \\ k &\triangleq \frac{\tau}{\lambda}\\ \end{aligned} \]

'Spatial angular frequency' or 'angular wave number' (number of equal phase locations per meter)

$\frac{\partial^2y}{\partial x^2} = y(x)frac{(-\omega)^2}{c^2}$ : Equation of Motion (Time Harmonic)

$y = A e^{ikx} + B e^{ikx}$ : General Solution $k \triangleq \frac{\tau}{\lambda}$ 'Spatial angular frequency' or 'angular wave number' (number of equal phase locations per meter)

$\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2}\left(A e^{ikx} + B e^{-ikx}\right) = \frac{-\omega^2}{c^2}\left(A e^{ikx} + B e^{ikx}\right)$

$-k^2A e^{ikx} - k^2 B e^{-ikx} = \frac{-\omega^2}{c^2}\left(A e^{ikx} + B e^{ikx}\right)$

$-k^2\left(A e^{ikx} + B e^{-ikx}\right) = \frac{-\omega^2}{c^2}\left(A e^{ikx} + B e^{ikx}\right)$ divide by $A e^{ikx} + B e^{ikx}$

$-k^2 = \frac{-\omega^2}{c^2}$

$-k^2c^2 = -\omega^2$

$k^2c^2 = \omega^2$

$\omega^2 = k^2c^2$ sqrt both sides

$\omega = kc$: dispersion relation and $k = \frac{\omega}{c}$

$y(x,t) = y(x)e^{i\omega t}$

$y(x,t) = Ae^{ik t}e^{i\omega t} + Be^{-ik t}e^{i\omega t}$

$y(x,t) = Ae^{i(\omega t + kx)} + Be^{i(\omega t - kx)}$

Boundary conditions

String length has been infinite

What if string length is finite

Assumption at ends of string: $y(0,t) = 0$ and $y(L,t) = 0$ $∀t$ (rigid condition)

\[ \begin{aligned} \require{color} \require{cancel} y(x,t) &= Ae^{i(\omega t + kx)} + Be^{i(\omega t - kx)} \\ y(0,t) &= Ae^{i(\omega t + k0)} + Be^{i(\omega t - k0)} \\ y(0,t) &= Ae^{i(\omega t)} + Be^{i(\omega t)} = 0 \\ Ae^{i(\omega t)} &= -Be^{i(\omega t)} \\ A\cancel{e^{i(\omega t)}} &= -B\cancel{e^{i(\omega t)}} \\ A &= -B \\ y(x,t) &= Ae^{i(\omega t + kx)} + Be^{i(\omega t - kx)} \\ y(x,t) &= Ae^{i(\omega t + kx)} - Ae^{i(\omega t - kx)} \\ y(x,t) &= Ae^{i\omega t}\left(e^{ikx} - e^{ikx}\right) \quad \text{Remember:} \frac{e^{i\theta} - e^{-i\theta}}{2i} = sin\theta \\ y(x,t) &= -2iAe^{i\omega t}sinkx: \text{ if } sinkx = 0 \text{ then } y(x,t) = 0 \\ \end{aligned} \]

The Other Boundary

if $sinkL = 0$ then $kL$ must be an integer muliple of $\pi$ i.e. $kL = n\pi$

\[\begin{aligned} \require{color} y(L,t) &= 0 \\ y(x,t) &= -2iAe^{i\omega t}sinkx \\ kL &= n\pi$ : $k = \frac{\omega}{c} \\ \frac{\omega}{c}L &= n\pi \\ \omega &= \frac{n\pi c}{L} \\ \omega_n &= \frac{n\pi c}{L} \\ \end{aligned} \]
